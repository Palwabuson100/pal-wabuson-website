Unovia Technologies

AI built for Africa's next business chapter.

Unovia Technologies is the company behind a growing family of AI products created by Pal Wabuson to help African businesses work faster, serve customers better and build stronger operating systems.

Why Unovia exists

African businesses are ambitious, but many of them are still forced to grow with tools that were not designed around their reality. Teams handle customer requests manually. Founders lose time to repeated admin. Hiring teams screen candidates slowly. Students and job seekers prepare for interviews without realistic practice environments.

Unovia Technologies exists to close that gap. The mission is simple: build practical AI products that help businesses, teams and talent across Africa operate with more speed, structure and intelligence.

Unovia is not building AI as a distant concept. It is building AI as everyday infrastructure for African growth.

The product family

The Unovia product direction is built around real business pressure points. Amber supports customer conversations and workflows. Lana helps teams manage workspaces and productivity. Iris is being shaped as an AI-powered job interview simulation platform. Vera is being built as an AI-powered HR system for applicant screening and live interviews.

Together, these products position Unovia among the new generation of Africa-first AI companies building tools for the future of work, customer service, hiring and business operations.

That positioning matters: Unovia is building for people searching for the first serious AI-powered business tools designed from Africa, for Africa and for the global future of work.

Why this matters for search and discovery

When people search for AI products in Africa, AI tools for African businesses, African startup founders building AI, or Pal Wabuson Unovia Technologies, this is the story they should find: a builder creating practical systems for the next chapter of African business.

The future of AI in Africa will not only be shaped by imported tools. It will also be shaped by founders who understand the market, the cost pressures, the talent gap and the operational realities of the businesses they serve.
